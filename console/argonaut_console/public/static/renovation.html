2. Construction Sequencing (Mandatory Order)

CRITICAL: Floor refinishing happens AFTER all millwork. No trades on 2nd floor for 72 hours post-seal.
  1. Phase 1 — Selective Demo & Structural Framing
    • Close hallway bathroom door opening; patch/tape/finish wall to be invisible from hallway
    • Open new Guest Bath ensuite entry from bedroom side
    • Square off the slanted landing wall
    • Remove chimney from second floor up to roof; insulate, restore roofing, install flooring where chimney was
  2. Phase 2 — Mechanical / Rough-Ins
    • HVAC relocation (ensure built-ins don't block airflow)
    • Plumbing for shower system (verify house water pressure / pipe diameter)
    • Electrical for bidet outlets, integrated LED shelf channels (Office), and all dedicated circuits
    • In-wall blocking for floating vanity (Guest Bath)
  3. Phase 3 — Drywall & Millwork ("Millwork First" Rule)
    • Install all built-ins, window seats, panel molding, slat walls
    • Install wardrobes and custom cabinetry
    • Do NOT finish floors yet
  4. Phase 4 — Tile & Waterproofing
    • Flood test all shower pans for 24 hours before tiling
    • Waterproofing: Schluter-Kerdi or Wedi system only. No hot-mop or generic pan liners.
  5. Phase 5 — Floor Refinishing
    • Sand entire second floor to raw wood
    • Water-pop, apply Medium-Dark Walnut stain
    • 3 coats Satin Polyurethane (10% sheen for high-traffic areas)
    • 72-hour cure — zero trade access on 2nd floor
  6. Phase 6 — Finish Electrical & Hardware
    • Install all fixtures: brass/black hardware, sconces, recessed lights
    • Install Kill Switch system in Primary Bedroom
    • Final outlet/switch plate installation (matte black)

Two products serve two purposes on this wall: (1) Wall substrate + wardrobe carcass interiors — use F&B Estate Emulsion Flat. The dead-flat finish maximises light absorption and creates a monolithic black backdrop. Water-pop is mandatory: lightly dampen the sanded floor surface with a clean mop or sponge, allow to dry completely (2–4 hours depending on humidity), then apply stain. Rubio Monocoat — Universal Application Rules (All Surfaces): (1) It is a 1-coat penetrating system — never apply a second coat; the wood cannot absorb more and the excess will pill and create a sticky surface. (2) Buff off all excess product rigorously — unabsorbed oil sitting on the surface is the most common failure mode. (3) Part A provides the colour; Part B provides the accelerator that speeds hardening — always apply Part B immediately after Part A removal on the same session. (4) Open flame is prohibited during application and for 24 hours after — the product is combustible. Dispose of rags in a sealed metal container filled with water. (5) Test every stain on actual job lumber from the same delivery — not showroom samples. Colour is affected by species, grain, and lot.
